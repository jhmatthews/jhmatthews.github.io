Hello!

I’m an astrophysics postdoctoral researcher working at the University of Oxford. My research involves understanding two main topics: the origins of ultra-high energy cosmic rays and the physics of outflows and jets from accreting systems. These two topics are inextricably linked.

Projects

Cosmic Ray Acceleration

It is not yet understood where the highest energy cosmic rays originate. One of my research areas involves investigating whether outflows from AGN can accelerate the highest energy cosmic rays.

MHD turbulence

Turbulence is a crucial aspect of cosmic ray acceleration, as it allows scattering of the particles across a shock front. I am also more generally interested in turbulence in astrophysical systems -- for example, as a means of pressure support in the ISM.

Disc Winds in Accreting Systems

Outflows are ubiquitous in accreting systems. I spent my PhD developing a Monte Carlo radiative transfer and photoionization code, and used it to synthesize spectra of accreting objects with associated outflows.

Quasar and AGN unification and orientation

It would be rather elegant if all accreting supermassive black holes could essentially be described by their mass, Eddington ratio and viewing angle. While countless other complications doubtless exist, I am especially interested in testing this paradigm. Part of this work involves searching for reliable orientation indicators, so we can constrain the geometry of absorbing and emitting plasma around the central engine. Image credit: Shen & Ho 2014.

Public Engagement

My public engagement work has involved taking this huge inflatable planetarium into local schools. As you can tell I get pretty excited about this. I also enjoy giving public talks. These are normally about alien life as that is the most popular topic for kids, but I've also given more specialised talks on winds and tori around black holes. This session included donuts.
