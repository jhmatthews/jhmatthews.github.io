James Matthews

Astronomy PhD Student at University of Southampton

My research involves using a Monte Carlo radiative transfer code to model accretion disk winds and the spectral features they produce. I am supervised by Christian Knigge.

The code is, slightly confusingly, called Python, but is written in C.
It was originally used to model Cataclysmic Variables, but has since been generalised to QSOs.

In particular my research involves seeing how many QSO features, such as the Broad Absorption Lines and Broad Emission Lines seen in spectra, can be explained by fairly simple biconical disk wind models. I also spend quite a bit of time testing and improving the implementation of Leon Lucy's macro atom scheme in the code, in the hope that it will improve our treatment of recombination lines.

In the past, I also worked with Rosanne Di Stefano at the Harvard-Smithsonian CfA on discovering exoplanets with microlensing, and wrote a masters thesis on the topic.
